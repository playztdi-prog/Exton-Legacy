Exton Through the Years

Late 1700s

European settlers, primarily of English and Welsh descent, began to establish farms and communities in the region during the Colonial Settlement and Revolutionary War era.

1859

The Exton Hotel was built at the intersection of the Chester Valley Railroad and the Lancaster Turnpike, serving as a stagecoach stop and later as a railroad station. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1983.

1868–1912

Yellow Springs served as the site of the Chester Springs Soldiers' Orphan School, providing education and care for children of Civil War soldiers.

1916–1952

The Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts operated a country school in Yellow Springs, contributing to the area's cultural development.

1937

Aerial photographs show Exton as a rural area with farmland and woods.

1973

The Exton Square Mall opened, marking a significant development in the region's commercial landscape.

1999

The mall underwent a major expansion, adding new anchor stores and increasing retail space.

Late 1990s

The Newcomen Society of the United States established its campus in Exton, featuring offices, a printing shop, library, museum, guest houses, a chapel, and a belltower with a carillon.

2011

The Exton Square Mall achieved sales per square foot of $332.

2016

Round One Entertainment opened a bowling and entertainment center in the former JCPenney space at Exton Square Mall.

2018

Whole Foods Market opened in Exton, further enhancing the area's retail offerings.

2024

A carnival at Exton Square Mall was canceled following a violent altercation, leading to the revocation of the event's permit.

2025

The Exton Square Mall was sold to Abrams Realty & Development for $34.25 million, with plans to redevelop the property into a mixed-use community featuring over 600 apartments and townhomes, along with shops, restaurants, fitness centers, entertainment venues, office space, and healthcare facilities.
